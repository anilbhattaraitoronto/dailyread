Re-Inhabiting the Earth

Anil Bhattarai

17 November 2018, Saturday

Bruno Latour is perhaps one of the most famous social scientists living today. For over five decades, he has been arguing that social scientists have not paid adequate attention to the active non-human constituents of the material world. This neglect has limited the efficacy of their work in illuminating the world humans have occupied. For long, they have been operating under an illusion that, somehow, the human world can be explained without taking into account the other active material constituents. Read more

About Nepal Reviewed

Recently, there has been a phenomenal growth of academic writings on Nepal. This growth has occurred in all academic fields. In Nepal Reviewed, we strive to present some of those writings in publicly accessible forms.

We believe that, while scholars have a lot to contribute to enriching our understanding about changing realities of Nepal, that understanding can be widely distributed if what scholars write becomes more accessible to the public.
